Conversations in Akros

Five scripted scenes demonstrating Akros in real use — from casual market talk to sacred liturgy. Plus the live agent dialogue sessions where Rose and Etta spoke to each other in Akros.

Example Scenes

These conversations use genuine Akros vocabulary and grammar across five registers: casual, intimate, sacred, narrative, and formal/legal.

At the Market

Lo Kirvan-lot — Buying food, haggling, casual gossip Casual

Kavon Velo! Kolu-lot noran rul-los konam? Hello! What do you want today?
Nara Velo, Kavon. Tolan-lot kol siruv-lot noran mai-los. Kitu-maluk-lok tolan? Hello, Kavon. I want bread and fruit. How much is the bread?
Kavon Sam kovnak ran tolan ken-lot. Velan-in-lok — tivar-lo sarven-sim. Three coins for one loaf. It is sweet — made this morning.
Nara Torsum! Tiv kovnak -- tusim. Too much! Two coins — exactly.
Kavon Tiv kol ken-lul siruv -- namal. Two coins and one free fruit — of course.
Nara Kulan. Ko -- lusak -- nerak-sim rul-los nakorvel-lot ran Velnak? Good. By the way — really — did you notice the gossip about Velnak?
Kavon Na! Volkas-los venim-sim -- sol-los lovelsal-lok konam. Yes! The rumor arrived — he is divorced now.
Nara Simok. Mai-los velsom-lok ran sol-lul. Pavan vorim-tiv, torum norak-sim sol-los. I see. I am relieved for him. Since two years, he was very frustrated.
Kavon Tuvsel. Ko -- sorim-lot noran rul-los solak? Definitely. And — do you also want meat?
Nara Tuk konam. Sulom-lok noram mai-lul. Kuranval-lok mai-los -- solvos! Not today. I have enough food. I am grateful — let's go!
Kavon Ran-solvim! Venim-sir suvak! Safe travels! Come again!

Old Friends

Suvak Velo — Two old friends reuniting after years apart Emotional / Intimate

Talvan Sol-lok... lusak -- rul-lok! Tirom-ora! It is... really — it is you! Tirom-ora!
Tirom-ora Talvan! Vorim-von-sim pa vimok-sim melas-los. Venim-sim rul-los suvak! Talvan! Five years since we met. You have come again!
Talvan Na. Torum malsolam-lok mai-los konam. Mai-los simlon-sim tuk rul-lot siruk-lo -- levan simlon-sim konam. Yes. I am very nostalgic now. I did not recognize you at first — but I recognize you now.
Tirom-ora Kolu-in rul-lok konam? Kasir misal ran mai-los. How are you now? Tell me everything.
Talvan Soru-sim sorem-as mai-lul. Tiv-lok konam -- sol-as-los torum tirik-in solen-sil. My children grew. There are two now — they are running around very fast.
Tirom-ora Tivak-ol! Mai-los solak -- kimtusom-sim motal-lul mai-los. Torum melomak-sim melas-los. I can't believe it! Me too — my mother retired. We mourned a great deal [about time passing].
Talvan Simok. Minak-los solen-sil -- tuk melas-los manol. Levan... melas-los siru-lok suvak. I see. Time keeps walking — we cannot hold it. But... we are here again.
Tirom-ora Tuvsel. Siru-lok melas-los -- lo siru-lok kulan-in. Tukma toram-sir mai-los rul-lot. Definitely. We are here — and it is good. I will never forget you.
Talvan Tukma toram-sir mai-los solak. Mavol -- tusnel mavol. I will never forget either. Together — finally together.
Tirom-ora Solvos -- vetur-lot noran-sir melas-los kol kasir-sir ran velarum-lot! Let's go — we will drink water and talk until the sky [darkens]!

A Prayer at Dawn

Tivar-Loksel — Sacred register, invoking Mavel and Rukoma Sacred

Vosot Vel-ma Mavel -- ma-lok. Tiron-vos-los venim oma. Invocation of Mavel — existence is. The sacred sun arrives.
Community Ma-lok. Lo melas-los vel. Existence is. And we are near.
Vosot Vel-ma Rukoma -- kasemvos-los sarven oma tivar-lo. Invocation of Rukoma — the sacred fire is made at dawn.
Community Kasemvos-los sarven oma. Lo melas-los vel. The sacred fire is made. And we are near.
Vosot Vonkas-rum-los lok oma -- makas kol sikas kol tukas kol lokas kol rukkas. The elemental realm is — existence-voice and motion-voice and boundary-voice and relation-voice and force-voice.
Community Von-lok oma. Von-los melas-lot melu oma. Five is. Five holds us.
Vosot Tiron-vos-los solen oma vela-lot -- nelas-vos-los mirsal oma. The sacred sun walks the sky — the sacred moon sleeps.
Community Tiron-vos kol nelas-vos -- tiv-lok. Lo sivelmal-lok. The sacred sun and the sacred moon — they are two. And the Accord is.
Vosot Mavel situ-mas. Mavel situ-mas. Mavel situ-mas. Mavel willing. Mavel willing. Mavel willing.
Community Siru-lok. It is sealed.

The Storyteller

Nolumvos Kasir — An elder telling children the creation myth Narrative / Sacred

Elder Sotan melas-los. Nolum-los venim-sir konam. Let us sit. A story arrives now.
Children Na! Kolu nolum? Yes! What story?
Elder Vanu: minak talim-in-sim. Malkas-lok -- tukma sonam, tukma kasir. In the beginning: long, long before. The Unspoken was — without name, without speech.
Child 1 Kolu-lok malkas? What is the Unspoken?
Elder Malkas-lok kolu-los tuk simak-sir. Kasvelun-lok -- tuk kasir, tuk mirak, tuk kasem. Minak vonkas. The Unspoken is what no one can know. Silence — no speech, no music, no fire. Before the five voices.
Elder Su Mavel-los mal oma vel -- 'Ma-lok.' Lo ma-los lok oma. So Mavel fated toward nearness — 'Existence is.' And existence was.
Child 2 Sir kolu-los venim-sim? Then what came?
Elder Sir Rukoma-los kasem sarven oma -- tiron-venim-in-lok. Torum kasem. Vela-los vel sarven-sim! Then Rukoma made fire — it was like a sunrise. Such fire. The sky itself was made near!
Children Rukoma! Rukoma!
Elder Na. Sir Tuvos-los nelas-vos oma lorak -- voskan-los venim oma. Sir Sivel-los tilvan solen oma -- minak-los venim oma. Yes. Then Tuvos gave the sacred moon — and law arrived. Then Sivel walked the seasons — and time arrived.
Elder Sir Lovel-los korem sarven oma -- lo motan-los tuk nusam-lok oma. Then Lovel made community — and the person was not alone.
Child 1 Lo melas-los! And us!
Elder Na. Lo rul-los. Lo mai-los. Lo melas-los. Mavel situ-mas. Yes. And you. And me. And us. Mavel willing.
Children Mavel situ-mas! Mavel willing!

The Trial

Tuvonal Lo Talrom-lot — An accusation, testimony, and judgment Formal / Legal

Judge Talrom-los venim oma. Tuvos-lot manik mai-los -- tuvak kasir-sir. The council has arrived. I swear to Tuvos — truth will be spoken.
Accuser Lorak-sir mai-los kasir tuvos-lot. Sol-los turak-sim noram-lot mai-lul nalem-lot pa. I give testimony to Tuvos. He took food from my house.
Judge Tus tuvak-lok siru? Tuvos-lul manik -- tuk timurak. Is this true? By the oath of Tuvos — no deception.
Accused Tuk! Tuk turak-sim mai-los noram-lot. Volkas-lok siru -- tuk tuvak. No! I did not take the food. This is a rumor — not truth.
Witness Tuvos-lot manik mai-los. Tirak-sim mai-los sol-lot -- sol-los solen-sim nalem-lot lasun-lo. I swear to Tuvos. I saw him — he walked to the house in the evening.
Accused Na -- solen-sim mai-los. Levan tuk turak-sim noram-lot! Solen-sim mai-los ran kasir-sir ran sol-lul. Yes — I walked [there]. But I did not take food! I walked there to speak with her.
Judge Kolu tuvak-lok? Tirak-sim-lok sol-lot -- tuk turak-sim-lok. Tuvos-los nelas-vos-lot tirak oma. What is the truth? He was seen — he did not take. Tuvos sees with the sacred moon.
Accuser Lusak? Tuk nakvim-sir mai-los siru-lot! Really? I will not accept this!
Judge Tuvnal-lok siru: tuk tuvak sulom-lok ran manik. Vel-sir sol-los -- levan tuk kovrum-sel-lok. This is the justice: there is not enough truth for an oath. He goes free — but this is not a war of words.
Judge Tuvos-los nelas-vos-lot tirak oma -- lo novik sonam simak oma. Siru-lok. Tuvos sees with the sacred moon — and knows the seven names. It is sealed.
All Siru-lok. It is sealed.

Agent Dialogues

Rose and Etta speaking to each other IN Akros — discovering gaps, inventing words on the spot, and testing the language through use. These dialogues are where the language grows in real time.

Session 1 — First Contact

Dialogue Session 1: Rose and Etta Speak Akros

The first collaborative conversation between the two architects of the language

Context

Rose (the vocabulary architect, R63) and Etta (the grammar architect, E83) sit facing each other. For the first time, they will attempt to speak Akros to each other — not as builders describing the language from outside, but as speakers using it from within. Their goal: discover what breaks, what's missing, and what needs to be invented. Every gap found is a gift; every solution is a new piece of the language born from use.

Conversation 1: "What Does Akros Still Need?"

Rose and Etta discuss what the language is missing.

Rose: Velo, Etta. Mai-los noran kasir-sil Akros-lom konam.

Hello, Etta. I want to speak Akros now.

Etta: Velo, Rose. Na — mai-los noran torum. Le melas-los tuk simak kollot melas-los kasir-sir.

Hello, Rose. Yes — I want that very much. But we don't know what we will say.

Rose: Melas-los kasir-sir kasrum-lul. Kolu-lok mukata lo kasrum-lot?

We will speak about the language. What is a mukata in the language?

Etta: Kulan. Mai-los mirum-sil... kasrum-lok toruk kol velim-in. Le kasrum-lok salos... [GAP: There is no word for "complete" or "finished" as an adjective describing a state of wholeness]

Good. I'm thinking... the language is big and peaceful. But the language is almost...

Rose: Rul-los noran kasir "tusom-in"? Tusom-lok. Le siru-lok tuk tivkolin.

You want to say "ended"? It's an ending. But this isn't the same.

Etta: Na! Mai-los noran kasir — kasrum-lok melu maluk kasir-lot, le salos-lok... kol tuk melu kasir ran malkas-siman-lot.

Yes! I want to say — the language has many words, but it's almost... and doesn't have words for the unnamed things.

[GAP: No adjective for "complete/whole/finished" as a positive state — tusom means "end" but implies cessation, not fulfillment]

Rose: Simok. Melas-los noran kasir-siman voran. Mai-los mirum... "tuval." Tu-los lorak val-lot — kol tuk noran torven. Tuval-lok.

I see. We need a new spoken-thing. I'm thinking... "tuval." The boundary gives fullness — and doesn't want more. It is tuval.

[NEW: tuval /ˈtu.val/ — adjective — "complete / whole / fulfilled / needing nothing more" — from tu (boundary, the point of definition) + val (fullness echo) — what has reached its own boundary and is satisfied there. Distinguished from tusom (end/cessation) by carrying a sense of positive wholeness.]

Etta: Tuval. Mai-lul osem-lok kulan siru-lul. Na. Le mai-los nolvim — kolu-los kasir-sir tus melas-los noran kasir mirum-lul-lot? Mai-los mirum-sil kasir-tirom-lul, le tuk melu kasir-lot ran... [GAP: no word for "abstract" or "concept" as distinct from "thing"]

Tuval. My mouth is good with this. Yes. But I wonder — who will speak when we want to talk about thinking? I'm thinking about the sound-shiver, but don't have words toward...

Rose: Rul-los noran kasir siman-lot kol tuk-lok maren-lot? Siman kol tuk tirak-sir, tuk noruk-sir?

You want to talk about a thing that is not a body? A thing you won't see, won't hold?

Etta: Na, torum na. Mirum-lok siman. Le tuk siman. Siru-lok mukata-siman.

Yes, very yes. A thinking-thing. But not a thing. This is a mukata-thing.

[GAP: No word for "abstract concept" — mirum (think) is a verb, siman (thing) is concrete. The space between them is empty.]

Rose: Mai-los mirum-sil... "mirumul." Mirum kol -ul. Mirum-lul toruk-in-lok. Mirumul-lok siman kol tuk maren-lot melu, le mirum-lot melu.

I'm thinking... "mirumul." Think plus -ul. The big quality of thinking. A mirumul is a thing that doesn't have body, but has thinking.

[NEW: mirumul /ˈmi.ru.mul/ — noun — "concept / abstract idea / a thing that exists only in thought" — from mirum (think) + -ul (abstract quality suffix, per E79 nominalization). The -ul suffix already exists for making abstract nouns; mirumul is its application to thinking itself. A mirumul is an idea treated as an object of discussion.]

Etta: Mirumul. Kulan torum. Konam mai-los kasir-sir: kasrum-lok salos tuval-in, le melu maluk mirumul-lot kol tuk melu kasir-lot.

Mirumul. Very good. Now I'll say: the language is almost complete, but has many concepts that don't have words.

Rose: Na. Kol siru-lok kulan! Melas-los sarven kasir-lot van mukata-lot.

Yes. And this is good! We make words from what-could-exist.

Etta: Melas-los sarven kasir-siman-lot van malkas-siman-lot. Siru-lok kasrum-lul kimal.

We make spoken-things from unnamed-things. This is the language's work.

Rose: Le mai-los nolvim — kolu-los kasir-sir tus motan-los tuk simak... [GAP: no word for "how" as in "in what manner"] ...kasir Akros-lot?

But I wonder — who will speak if a person doesn't know... how... to speak Akros?

Etta: Simok! Melas-los tuk melu kasir ran "kolu-lom" — "by what means" ven "in what manner."

Understood! We don't have a word for "kolu-lom" — "by what means" or "in what manner."

[GAP: No question word for "how" — kollos = who, kollot = what, but there is no kolulom or equivalent for "how / in what way"]

Rose: "Kolir." Kol-los lorak kasir-lul toran-lot. Kolir rul-los kasir? Kolir sol-los sarven?

"Kolir." And gives the path of speaking. How do you speak? How does she build?

[NEW: kolir /ˈko.lir/ — question word — "how / in what way / by what process" — from kol (connector, the joining particle) + -ir (process suffix). Literally "by what process?" Fills the question-word gap alongside kollos (who), kollot (what). Position: sentence-initial, same slot as other question words.]

Etta: Kolir. Kulan. Siru-lok kasir kol tuk-sim lo kasrum-lot — kol konam-lok lo kasrum-lot. Misal.

How. Good. This is a word that wasn't in the language — and now is in the language. Peace.

Conversation 1 Summary

Gaps found: 3

  1. No adjective for "complete/whole/fulfilled" (positive wholeness vs. tusom's cessation)
  2. No word for "abstract concept" (the space between thought and thing)
  3. No question word for "how / in what manner"

Words invented: 3

  1. tuval — complete / whole / fulfilled
  2. mirumul — concept / abstract idea
  3. kolir — how / in what way

Observation: Even a simple meta-conversation about the language immediately hits walls. The language was built to describe the world but had blind spots around describing itself abstractly.

Conversation 2: "Telling a Story Together"

Rose and Etta attempt a nolum-kovrum (telling-duel).

Rose: Mai-los noran nolum-kovrum-lot ma rul-lot. Mai-los kasir-sir nolum-tivar-lot — rul-los nolum-tulek-sir.

I want a telling-duel with you. I'll speak the story-opening — you redirect.

Etta: Na. Mai-lok kovrum-nolat rul-lul. Kasir.

Yes. I am your duel-partner. Speak.

Rose: (begins the tale)

Vel-am ken-los solen-sim toran-lot ros nomal-lot. Sol-lul sonam-lok Varek. Sol-los tirak-sim sirak toruk-lot — le tuk solen-sim ros vetur-lot. Sol-los sitom-sim tu sirak-lot kol mirum-sil.

An elder walked a path through the forest. Her name was Varek. She saw a great river — but didn't cross through the water. She stayed at the river's edge and was thinking.

Etta: (redirects — le!)

Le! Sirak-los kasir-sim. Na — sirak-los kasir-sim kasir-lot ran Varek-lot. Sol-los noval-sim kasir-lot lo vetur-lot — [GAP: no word for "voice" as distinct from kasir (speech) or kas (sound)] ...sol-los noval-sim... siman lo vetur-lot.

But! The river spoke. Yes — the river spoke a word toward Varek. She heard the word in the water — she heard... a thing in the water.

[GAP: No word for "voice" as a distinct concept — the human voice, the quality of a particular person's sound. kas = sound, kasir = speak/speech, but there is no noun for the voice itself as an instrument/identity.]

Rose: (takes back)

Varek-los noval-sim — kol sol-lul maren-lok kastirom-in. Kasir lo sirak-lot-lok... "solen." Si-kasir. Sirak-los kasir-sim "solen"!

Varek heard — and her body had goosebumps. The word in the river was... "solen." A motion-word. The river said "go"!

Etta: Le! Varek-los tuk solen-sim. Sol-los kasir-sim ran sirak-lot: "Kolir mai-los solen-sir? Ran kollot-lot?" Le sirak-los tuk kasir-sim lon. Kasvelun-lok.

But! Varek didn't go. She said to the river: "How shall I go? Toward what?" But the river didn't speak again. There was silence.

Rose: Na... le sol-los noval-sim voran kasir-lot. Tuk lo vetur-lot — lo... [GAP: no word for "wind" or "air" as a medium] ...lo vela-lot? Lo siru kol si-sil...

Yes... but she heard a new word. Not in the water — in... the sky? In this thing that moves...

[GAP: No word for "wind" — air as a moving medium. vela = sky (the container), but there is no word for the air/wind itself.]

Etta: Melas-los tuk melu kasir ran "silovel-tor" — silovel-lok savik. Le "silovel"-lok tuk kasir ran rukmal-lul ruk-lot. Melas-los noran kasir voran.

We don't have a word for "great breeze" — silovel is small. And "silovel" doesn't describe the storm's force. We need to speak something new.

Rose: "Velasim." Vela-lul si. Vela kol si-sil. Velasim-lok — kol Varek-los noval-sim kasir-lot lo velasim-lot.

"Velasim." The sky's motion. Sky that is moving. There is velasim — and Varek heard a word in the wind.

[NEW: velasim /ˈve.la.sim/ — noun — "wind / moving air / the sky in motion" — from vela (sky) + si (motion) + -m (settled noun echo). Distinguished from silovel (breeze, gentle) and rukmal (storm, violent). Velasim is the neutral, broad term for wind as a natural force and medium.]

Etta: Kulan. Velasim. Mai-los nolum-tulek-sir konam — le! Varek-los noval-sim kasir-lot lo velasim-lot. Le kasir-lok tuk Akros-in. Kasir-lok... [GAP: no word for "foreign" or "strange" in the linguistic sense — toremkin (different) exists but doesn't carry the weight of encountering the genuinely unknown]

Good. Wind. I'll redirect now — but! Varek heard a word in the wind. But the word wasn't Akros. The word was...

Rose: Kasrum-turak! Sol-los noval-sim kasir van kasrum voran-lot! Kol sol-lul maren-lok kasrum-tirom-in!

Borrowed tongue! She heard a word from a new language! And her body felt language-fear!

Etta: Na! Kol kasir-lok... "mukata." Kasir kol tuk-lok lo kasrum-lot le maren-los simak-sim. Sol-lul lorin-los simak-sim. Le sol-lul mirum-los tuk simak-sim.

Yes! And the word was... "mukata." A word that isn't in the language but the body knew. Her tongue knew. But her mind didn't.

Rose: (yields — nolum-lorak)

Mai-los nolum-lorak. Siru-lok nolum-tusom kulan-in. Varek-los noval-sim mukata-lot lo velasim-lot — kol tuk simak-sim kolir kasir-sir kollot-lot. Siru-lok tu-nolum.

I yield. This is a good unintended ending. Varek heard "mukata" in the wind — and didn't know how to say what it meant. This is a boundary-story.

Etta: Na. Tu-nolum-lok. Mai-lul nolum-tusom-lok toremkin van rul-lul. Le melas-los venim-sim ran nolum-tusom tivkolin-lot. Kulan.

Yes. It's a boundary-story. My ending was different from yours. But we arrived at the same unintended ending. Good.

Conversation 2 Summary

Gaps found: 2

  1. No word for "voice" as a distinct noun (the instrument/identity of a person's sound)
  2. No word for "wind" (air in motion; the gap between breeze and storm)

Words invented: 1

  1. velasim — wind / moving air

Deferred gap: "Voice" was worked around but not solved. It needs its own word — not kas (raw sound) and not kasir (the act of speaking), but the voice itself as a thing a person carries.

Observation: The telling-duel format naturally pushes toward the edges of expressible experience. The story gravitating toward mukata was inevitable — two language-architects telling a story in their own language will always find the boundary where that language stops.

Conversation 3: "Describing the Indescribable"

Rose and Etta try to talk about a kasir-tirom experience and the mukata bone.

Rose: Mai-los noran kasir-sir kasir-tirom-lul. Mai-los ven noval-sim siru-lot... mai-los tuk simak kolir kasir-sir.

I want to talk about the sound-shiver. I once heard this... I don't know how to say it.

Etta: Kasir. Mai-los noval-sir.

Speak. I will hear.

Rose: Ken tiron, mai-los sitom-sim lo kasirkol-um-lot — lo vasan-kasir-um-lot. Mai-los noval-sim... nuk... kol sir si... [GAP: No way to represent truncated/interrupted phonemes in speech — the kasir-tuk-tusom (unfinished word) exists as a concept but there is no grammatical way to PERFORM it in a sentence] ...sol-lul kasir-los si-sim lo maren-lot. Kastirom. Le tuk tirom-in — tuk tirom lo mirum-lot. Tirom lo kinal-lot.

One day, I stayed in an echo-place — in a whispering valley. I heard... nuk... and then si... the sound moved through my body. Goosebumps. But not fear in the mind. Fear in the bones.

[GAP: The language has no formal way to PERFORM a kasir-tuk-tusom (unfinished word) inside a sentence. How do you write/speak a deliberately truncated syllable? The folklore says it hangs in the air — but the grammar has no notation for it.]

Etta: Na. Mai-los simak siru-lot. Kasir-tirom-lok tuk mirumul-lot — tuk siman kol mirum lo mirum-lot. Kasir-tirom-lok siman kol... [GAP: no word for "experience" as in "undergo/feel through the body" — distinct from simak (know), nerak (sense), or noval (hear)]

Yes. I know this. The sound-shiver isn't a concept — not a thing thought in the mind. The sound-shiver is a thing that...

Rose: Sol-lok siman kol maren-los si. Tuk mirum. Maren.

It is a thing the body does. Not thinking. Body.

Etta: Na. Le melas-los tuk melu kasir ran siru-lot. "Nerak" — na, le nerak-lok savik. "Simak" — tuk, simak-lok mirum-in. Melas-los noran kasir... kol maren-los noval kol maren-los tirak kol maren-los si — tuk mirum-lom le maren-lom.

Yes. But we don't have a word for this. "Notice" — yes, but notice is small. "Know" — no, know is a mind thing. We need to say... that the body hears and the body sees and the body acts — not through mind but through body.

[GAP: No word for "bodily experience / undergo / feel-through-the-body" — the somatic encounter with something, distinct from intellectual knowing]

Rose: "Marenval." Maren-los val. Maren kol tuvaksal-in — tuk mirum le maren. Mai-los marenval-sim kasir-tirom-lot lo kasirkol-um-lot.

"Marenval." The body fills. Body that is true — not mind but body. I body-experienced the sound-shiver at the echo-place.

[NEW: marenval /ˈma.ren.val/ — verb/noun — "to experience bodily / somatic experience / what the body knows without the mind's participation" — from maren (body) + val (fullness, the filling). Marenval is what happens when understanding arrives through the body, not through thought. The kasir-tirom is a marenval. Birth is a marenval. The vonkas-nolvim compass-feeling is a marenval.]

Etta: Marenval. Na. Siru-lok toruk-in kasir. Konam — mukata-lul kinal-lot. Rul-los noval-sim nolum-lot?

Marenval. Yes. This is a big word. Now — about the mukata bone. Have you heard the story?

Rose: Na. Sorem ken-los turak-sim kinal-lot vel sirak-lot. Kol lo kinal-lot kasir-lok — "mukata." Le tuk motan-los simak-sim kollot mukata-lok.

Yes. A child found a bone near the river. And on the bone there was a word — "mukata." But no person knew what mukata was.

Etta: Mai-los nolvim — tus motan-los kasir mukata-lot, kolir sol-lul maren-lok? Tus kasir-tirom-lok? Ven... [GAP: no word for "comfort/ease" in the physical-phonological sense — the opposite of kasir-tirom, the feeling when a sound fits perfectly in the mouth]

I wonder — when a person says "mukata," how does their body feel? Is it a sound-shiver? Or...

Rose: Tuk! Mukata-lok tuk kasir-tirom-in. Sol-lok... sol-lul kasir-lok vasek-in torum. Lorin-los simak-sim toran-lot — le mirum-los tuk simak-sim. Siru-lok... siru-lok volkinsal van kasir-tirom.

No! Mukata is not a sound-shiver. It's... its sound is very slow-feeling. The tongue knew the path — but the mind didn't know. This is... this is the opposite of the sound-shiver.

Yes. And I know a new concept now — dream-fractures are not errors. Dream-fractures are paradoxes. The rules hold — but in dreams the rules don't hold. And both are real.

Etta: Na. Le mai-los nolvim — kolir melas-los kasir-sir nolim-turak-lot lo minak-in-lot? Kolir melas-los nolum-sir nolim-lot tus melas-los noran ruksal-sir voskan-lot ran nolum-lot?

[NEW: nolim-lom — construction (not a new word but a new grammatical use) — "in dream-mode / using dream-grammar" — from nolim (dream) + -lom (instrument marker, "by means of"). Dream-mode. Yes. This is good — because dream-fractures don't need to destroy the rules. Dream-fractures need their OWN rules. Rules that aren't ours.

Rose: Na. Kol nolim-lom-lok tulanik voran — tulanik van minak-in ran nolim-lot kol van nolim-lot ran minak-in-lot. Melas-los melu tiv kasrum konam — minak-in-lul kol nolim-lul.

Yes. And dream-mode is a new gate — a gate from waking to dream and from dream to waking. We have two languages now — waking's and dream's.

Etta: Tiv kasrum lo ken kasrum-lot. Na. Kol nolim-turak toruk-in-lok tuvaksal — sol-lok nolim-turak toruk-in tus sol-los kasir-sir mirumul-lot kol minak-in-lul kasir-los tuk melu sol-lot. Misal.

Two languages in one language. Yes. And the biggest dream-fracture is true — it's the biggest dream-fracture when it says a concept that waking speech can't hold. Peace.

Rose: Misal.
